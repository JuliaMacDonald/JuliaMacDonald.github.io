The Upcoming Revolution of AI Becoming Our Teacher

RL Education

Where we are already:

AI is training the best chess players in the world:

A research team from DeepMind partnered with four world champions: Vladimir Kramnik (world champion 2000-2007), Dommaraju Gukesh (#11 best player in the world), Hou Yifan (women's world champion) and Maxime Vachier-Lagrave (#16 best player in the world).

First, the researchers extracted chess play concepts from AlphaZero - a model trained without any human supervision entirely on self-play - aka on Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards.

Next, the researchers identified concepts that were 1) novel - not present in any recorded human games + 2) “teachable to another AI agent with no prior knowledge of the concept” (which was tested by teaching these concepts to another AI model without any prior chess knowledge). What made these concepts unique? Many violated core chess rules - for example AlphaZero would sacrifice a queen to gain a subtle positional advantage 10 moves later.

Finally, researchers converted these concepts into chess puzzles which where given to the champions. The champions would attempt the puzzles - learn from AlphaZero and then attempt unseen puzzles with same underlying concepts.

All four champions improved. On average, they solved 0.85 more puzzles correctly out of every 4 they attempted resulting in a 21% improvement. Whether the champions learned by studying AlphaZero's specific moves or simply by being exposed to its concepts - AlphaZero enabled their learning in either case.

Where we will be soon:

AI systems will be able to:

  • Identify specific knowledge gaps any student has
  • Create custom tasks targeting each gap precisely
  • Test the student's knowledge and iterate

Every training system will adapt continuously to each student and will retain their progress. At each point, AI will decide if the learning needs to be more challenging, easier or taken in an entirely different direction -> think "the Mind Game" Ender Wiggin was playing.

All this can happen in a virtual 3D world (created by World Labs or by “Genie 5”?) with students wearing VR glasses and able to change their training world by just speaking out loud to it.

I wonder if this is what Andrej Karpathy is building at Eureka Labs?
