AI Speech Brain

Python Next.js + TypeScript Claude & GPT Obsidian 2026 · Active

A personal AI research brain I built for speech prep — it reads the firehose of AI research so I don't have to, and it learns my taste with every decision I make.

What it is

When I prepare talks — currently centered on the state of human data in AI — the raw material is scattered across YouTube talks, newsletters, papers, and long seed documents. AI Speech Brain is a pipeline that ingests all of those sources and distills them into atomic "nuggets": single, self-contained insights that are actually usable on stage.

How it works

sources → transcripts → nugget extraction → taste judging → top picks → my review → better judging
  • Ingestion — a watchlist of YouTube channels, RSS newsletters, and dropped-in documents is fetched daily, fully automatically.
  • Extraction — Claude turns each transcript into candidate nuggets.
  • Cross-vendor judging — a second model from a different provider (GPT) independently scores each nugget against my taste profile, so neither vendor's blind spots go unchecked.
  • Review — I review the top-ranked cards one at a time in a keyboard-driven web app (approve, reject-with-reason, edit, skip), or directly inside my Obsidian vault.
  • The loop — every approve/reject decision is synced back into the taste set, so tomorrow's judging is sharper than today's.

Why I like this design

It runs itself. A scheduled cloud job and a local daily job split the work between them, and the whole system converges on my judgment instead of a generic notion of "important." Three different surfaces — web app, terminal, Obsidian — all read and write the same data, so I can review from wherever I already am.

Built with Python, Next.js 14, TypeScript, Supabase, and the Claude and OpenAI APIs. The repository is private (it contains my personal research material) — happy to talk through the architecture: LinkedIn.
