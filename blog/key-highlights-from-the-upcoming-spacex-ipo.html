Key Highlights from the Upcoming SpaceX IPO

AI Industry Markets

SpaceX is going public as a combined entity incl SpaceX, Starlink and xAI (Grok + twitter). The IPO is scheduled for June 2026.

  • SpaceX will be fast-tracked so index funds (which hold most people’s 401ks) will be required to start buying SpaceX just 15 days after it lists - instead of the typical 6-10 months. So they’ll likely be buying near the top of the price range - before insiders start selling and before the market has settled on a stable price.
  • SpaceX S-1 prospectus lists the combined entity's total addressable market as 80% coming from Enterprise AI Applications. That presumably implies enterprises using Grok. According to Reuters Grok's enterprise adoption is currently around 0.2%. Elon tweeted recently that "xAI was not built right the first time around, so is being rebuilt from the foundations up." However Grok's compute facilities needed for training (Colossus and Colossus II data centers) are now rented out to Anthropic. Also all 11 non-Musk original xAI cofounders have reportedly left. Another option however is that SpaceX will end up acquiring Cursor (as its rumored to be in acquisition conversations right now) or a Cursor competitor to enable the projected Enterprise AI adoption.
  • 2 months ago SpaceX took a $20bn bridge loan. A sizable portion of the money raised in the IPO will go back to repaying it.
  • Of the 4 businesses only 1 is profitable (Starlink)
  • As of Dec 25 SpaceX had purchased $131M worth of Cybertrucks from Tesla
  • SpaceX will go public with a 2 class share structure: Class A sold to public with 1 vote per share. Class B shares held by Elon / other insiders will have 10 votes per share. The filing shows that Elon will retain 85.1% of the company's combined voting power.
