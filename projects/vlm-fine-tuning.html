VLM Fine-Tuning Lab

PyTorch Hugging Face LoRA / PEFT Apple Silicon 2026

Can you fine-tune a vision-language model on a MacBook? Yes — and doing it end-to-end yourself teaches you more about how these models work than a dozen blog posts.

What it is

A hands-on training project: taking an open vision-language model and teaching it visual question answering — show the model an image, ask "what color is the umbrella?", get a grounded answer. The whole workflow lives in a single reproducible notebook that runs locally on Apple Silicon.

What it does

dataset (VQAv2) → preprocess image+question pairs → LoRA fine-tune → evaluate answers
  • Dataset — a curated slice of VQAv2, the standard visual question-answering benchmark: real photos with human-written questions and answers.
  • Parameter-efficient training — instead of updating billions of weights, LoRA adapters train a tiny fraction of parameters, which is what makes fine-tuning feasible on a laptop GPU (Apple's MPS backend).
  • Full loop — hardware check, data inspection, training with the Hugging Face Trainer, and side-by-side evaluation of model answers before and after fine-tuning.

Why it matters to my work

I spend my days helping teams decide what AI can and can't do for them — including years running AI data operations, where training data quality was the product. Training a model with my own hands, on my own machine, keeps those recommendations honest: I know what a fine-tune costs, what it fixes, and where it quietly fails.

Built with PyTorch, Hugging Face Transformers/Datasets, and PEFT on Apple Silicon. Want to compare notes on small-scale fine-tuning? Find me on LinkedIn.
