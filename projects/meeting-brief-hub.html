Meeting Brief Hub

Next.js TypeScript Supabase 2026

I lead go-to-market, which means back-to-back calls - prospects, partners, customers. Every call produces follow-ups, objections, and context the next call depends on, and all of it lands in transcripts nobody reopens. Meeting Brief Hub turns those transcripts into precise follow-ups, meeting prep, and a current picture of every account and relationship - for the whole team, not just whoever was on the call.

What it does

When a call ends, the transcript arrives in a shared inbox via webhook (the app works with an AI meeting notetaker, plus a paste-in option for meetings without one). The app reads the transcript and drafts a proposal: follow-up tasks, updates to the account brief, objections that came up, key deal terms. A teammate reviews the proposal and clicks Apply. Only then does anything get written to the shared records.

Around that inbox there's a small workspace: a drag-and-drop board of every contact with next follow-up dates, one living brief per account (deal stage, what they want, recent activity), and a log of objections and key terms across meetings. There's also an "improve with AI" button for summaries - a person presses it, it never runs on its own.

Stack choices

Next.js (App Router) + TypeScript, Supabase for Postgres and auth, deployed on Vercel. Supabase because the whole data model is relational - accounts, people, meetings, tasks - and row-level security comes with it instead of being something you build. The webhook endpoint verifies signatures on every payload: transcripts are sensitive, and an open endpoint that writes toward your CRM is asking for trouble. The AI-assist step runs server-side only, so no key ever reaches the browser. And Vercel simply because a webhook needs a stable public URL, and I didn't want to babysit a server for that.
