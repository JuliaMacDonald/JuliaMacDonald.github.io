VLM Fine-Tuning Lab

PyTorch Hugging Face LoRA Apple Silicon 2026

I spent years running AI data operations, and I kept advising teams on fine-tuning decisions. At some point I decided I should do one end to end myself again, with my own hands, on my own laptop. So I fine-tuned a vision-language model on my MacBook.

The setup

The task is visual question answering: show the model a photo, ask "what color is the umbrella?", get a grounded answer. I trained LoRA adapters on a slice of VQAv2 (the standard VQA benchmark - real photos with human-written questions and answers) using the Hugging Face Trainer on Apple's MPS backend. LoRA means you only update a tiny fraction of the weights, which is the reason any of this runs on a laptop at all.

VQAv2 slice → preprocess image+question pairs → LoRA fine-tune → compare answers before/after

The whole thing lives in one notebook: hardware check, a look at the data, training, and a before/after comparison of the model's answers. Nothing hidden behind a framework.

Why bother

Because "you should fine-tune" and "you should fix your data" are very different invoices, and I hand out that advice for a living. Doing the training run myself keeps the advice honest - I know what a fine-tune costs, what it fixes, and where it quietly fails. It's a learning tool for me, not a product. That's the point.

PyTorch + Hugging Face Transformers/Datasets + PEFT, on Apple Silicon.
