How Do We Unlock Emotional Intelligence in AI?

Human Data Multimodal

So far AI progress has focused on two main goals: increasing capabilities and ensuring safety & alignment with human preferences. However alignment doesn't equal EQ. This difference might not always be clearly visible in text-only interactions, but it becomes significantly evident in voice and video contexts, which involve far greater degrees of freedom.

Consider a customer interacting with an AI agent who says, "Oh sure, this product is great." Should the agent respond differently if the customer sounds sarcastic? What if a job candidate interacts with an AI interviewer and keeps rolling their eyes, does that matter? These subtle human behaviors significantly impact our daily interactions and decision-making. We constantly interpret thousands of nuances in tone, facial expressions, and body language to understand and respond appropriately.

So how do we unlock emotional intelligence in AI?

The answer, I believe, as with other capabilities, lies in the training data. But not the type we've focused on so far.

We’ll need datasets that capture real emotions (facial expressions, tone changes, micro-reactions) combined with data on the situations that cause these emotions. We'll need complex trajectories such as situation → perceived outcome → emotional state → behavioral reaction. This kind of data will be expensive and hard to collect. It may even require new data modalities. For example, hardware might be part of the answer. The AI wearables currently in development by Meta and OpenAI could serve as passive data collectors - capturing voice tone, facial expressions + corresponding data on whats going on behind the scenes to cause these reactions (of course this assumes we'll figure out the privacy side of the matter and people will consent for usage of that data for training). Either way, I believe emotional intelligence won't emerge from better models alone, it will require different data.
