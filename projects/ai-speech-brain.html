AI Speech Brain

Python Next.js + TypeScript Claude + GPT 2026 · Active

I give talks on the state of human data in AI. The source material for a good talk is scattered across hours of YouTube panels, a pile of newsletters and papers I bookmark and rarely reopen. So I built a tool that does the collecting for me.

Why I built it

Prep for one talk used to mean 20+ hours of watching and reading, and most of that material never makes it on stage. The bottleneck was never the thinking. It was the triage.

How it works

Every day a pipeline pulls whatever is new from my watchlist (YouTube channels, RSS newsletters, documents I drop in). One model extracts candidate "nuggets" - single, self-contained insights. A second model from a different vendor scores each nugget against my taste profile. Two vendors on purpose: I don't want to inherit any one lab's blind spots.

watchlist → transcripts → nugget extraction → taste judging → top 8 cards → my review → sharper judging tomorrow

The day's top 8 land as cards in a small web app. I go through them with my coffee - thumbs up, thumbs down, edit, skip - about 10 minutes. Each card has a few tools attached: I can ask it any question, get a version in plain English, or pull more context from the source transcript. Every decision I make flows back into the taste profile, so the ranking keeps moving closer to what I would have picked myself.

What it doesn't do

It doesn't write my talks and it doesn't decide what matters - I do. The tool's whole job is to make sure that when I sit down to think, the 8 best candidates of the day are already on the table instead of buried at minute 47 of some panel recording.

Python + Next.js 14 + Supabase, with the Claude and OpenAI APIs. The repo is private - it contains my research material and taste data.
