Meeting Brief Hub

Next.js TypeScript Supabase Webhooks 2026

A hosted team workspace that turns meeting recordings into living account intelligence — so what was said on a call actually becomes follow-ups, tasks, and an up-to-date brief, instead of evaporating.

What it is

Teams that live on back-to-back calls have a familiar problem: the recorder captures everything, and then nothing happens with it. Meeting Brief Hub ingests transcripts from an AI meeting notetaker the moment a call ends, and routes them through a structured workflow:

recording → signed webhook → inbox (human review) → apply → accounts · people · tasks · objections · key terms

What it does

  • Living account briefs — each account has a single evolving page: deal stage, "what they want," and a timeline of activity.
  • People board — a drag-and-drop status board of every contact, with next follow-up dates so no relationship goes cold.
  • Inbox with a human gate — every new transcript arrives as a proposal. A teammate reviews and clicks Apply before anything touches the shared records; the AI never silently edits your CRM data.
  • Objections & key terms — recurring pushback and deal language get extracted and tracked across meetings, not lost inside individual calls.
  • Manual import — notes from meetings without a recorder can be pasted into the same pipeline.
  • Optional AI assist — a server-side "improve with AI" step sharpens summaries on demand, triggered by a person, never automatically.

The design principle

Ingestion creates proposals, not facts. Automation does the tedious 80% — parsing, structuring, drafting — and a human makes the 20% of calls that matter. That single rule is what makes the team trust the system enough to actually run on it.

Built with Next.js (App Router), TypeScript, Supabase (Postgres + Auth), and signature-verified webhooks, deployed on Vercel. The repository is private (it holds team data) — happy to talk through the build: LinkedIn.
